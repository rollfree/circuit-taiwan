Circuit Taiwan is defined by its outputs, not just the journey. Every kilometer generates unique data that currently does not exist in public domains. This post details the four core dimensions of our research architecture.

## 1. Medical Telemetry
We are recording the physiological cost of mobility.
*   **Sensors:** Continuous heart rate, skin temperature, and galvanic skin response.
*   **Goal:** To understand thermoregulation and fatigue in subtropical conditions for wheelchair users.
*   **Output:** A dataset for adaptive sports medicine and wearable tech validation.

## 2. Accessibility Mapping
We are auditing the route at wheelchair height.
*   **Data:** Surface quality, gradient percentages, shelter locations, and charging points.
*   **Goal:** To create the first systematic accessibility map of Taiwan's coastal highways.
*   **Output:** Open-source Creative Commons data for urban planners and travel platforms.

## 3. Safety Engineering
We are validating hardware in real-world extremes.
*   **System:** Integrated lightning and weather early-warning system.
*   **Goal:** To test vulnerability alerts for low-speed road users in mountainous coastal zones.
*   **Output:** Technical reports for IoT sponsors and open-source hardware communities.

## 4. Cultural Documentation
We are archiving the human story.
*   **Focus:** Indigenous communities along the coast.
*   **Output:** A trilingual archive created with community consent.

## Open Data
All non-sensitive data will be published under Creative Commons licenses upon completion. This project is not just about the journey; it is about the data we leave behind.

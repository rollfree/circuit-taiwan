Circuit Taiwan is an adaptive mobility expedition circumnavigating Taiwan's coast. Our 1,200 km route passes through traditional territories of several indigenous nations. 

![Taiwan Tribes Traversed](/images/Taiwan_Indigenous_Accessible_Travel_Map.jpg)

We approach these territories as **guests**, not as cultural documentarians. Our primary goal is safe passage and expedition success. Cultural engagement is secondary and will follow protocols **if and when provided** by communities or the Council of Indigenous Peoples.

<!--more-->

## Indigenous Territories on Our Route

We will cross or pass near territories of **6 officially recognized indigenous peoples**:

### East Coast (North to South)

| Tribe | Chinese Name | Route Segment | Notes |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **Truku** (Taroko) | 太魯閣族 | Su-Hua Highway | Sacred mountains, Taroko Gorge area |
| **Amis** | 阿美族 | Hualien to Taitung | Largest indigenous nation; coastal communities |
| **Puyuma** (Pinuyumayan) | 卑南族 | Taitung area | Traditional boat-building culture |
| **Paiwan** | 排灣族 | Southern coast | Slate architecture, glass bead craftsmanship |

### West Coast (South to North)

| Tribe | Chinese Name | Route Segment | Notes |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **Paiwan** | 排灣族 | Pingtung/Kenting | Ancient tribal territories |
| **Rukai** | 魯凱族 | Southern coast | Mountain-coastal interface |
| **Plains Indigenous** | 平埔族群 | Kaohsiung/Tainan | Historical territories; cultural revitalization |

**Note:** We will NOT visit Orchid Island (Tao/Yami), Sun Moon Lake (Thao), or mountain tribes (Atayal, Bunun, Tsou) as these are outside our coastal route.

## Our Approach: Respectful but Clear

### What We Will Do

✅ **Notify** the Council of Indigenous Peoples (CIP) and relevant tribal councils before the expedition  
✅ **Request** cultural protocols if they exist for passage through traditional territories  
✅ **Follow** any protocols that are clearly communicated to us  
✅ **Respect** "no photography" or "no entry" requests without question  
✅ **Acknowledge** traditional territories in our content and communications  

### What We Will NOT Do

❌ **Extract** cultural knowledge or ceremonies for content  
❌ **Assume** we have rights to film or photograph people or sacred sites  
❌ **Demand** cultural performances or explanations  
❌ **Share revenue** from our content with tribes or organizations  
❌ **Transfer copyright** of our expedition materials to any party

## Coordination with Council of Indigenous Peoples

We will coordinate with the **Council of Indigenous Peoples (CIP)** (原住民族委員會):

- **Role**: Ministry-level body under Executive Yuan
- **Current Minister**: Ljaucu Zingrur
- **Our Request**: 
  - Guidance on appropriate protocols for passage through traditional territories
  - Introduction to relevant tribal councils
  - **Potential sponsorship or endorsement** of the expedition
  - Support for accessibility infrastructure documentation

**We seek CIP as a sponsor and advisor, not as a content controller.**

## What We Offer to Tribes & CIP

In exchange for protocol guidance and potential sponsorship, we offer:

### For Sponsors (CIP, Tribal Councils, Cultural Organizations)

- 🎯 **Brand visibility** on expedition materials (vehicle, website, documentary)
- 📊 **Accessibility data** for tribal tourism infrastructure (open-source, Creative Commons)
- 🌐 **Trilingual exposure** (Chinese/French/English) to international audiences
- 📹 **Professional footage** of tribal areas (if permitted; delivered high-resolution)
- 🔗 **Connection** to Belgian/French media and cultural institutions

### What We Do NOT Offer

- ❌ Revenue sharing from content monetization
- ❌ Copyright transfer or co-ownership
- ❌ Editorial control over expedition narrative
- ❌ Guaranteed cultural content production
- ❌ Exclusive rights to expedition materials

## Protocol Request Process

### Before Departure (3-6 months prior)

1. **Contact CIP** with expedition overview and route details
2. **Request written protocols** for each tribal territory we cross
3. **Ask about sponsorship opportunities** for CIP and tribal councils
4. **Clarify copyright and content ownership** upfront
5. **Document all communications** for transparency

### If No Protocols Are Provided

If CIP or tribal councils do not respond or do not provide specific protocols:

- We will proceed with **general respect** for all communities
- We will **avoid photographing** people without explicit permission
- We will **not enter** clearly marked restricted areas
- We will **acknowledge** traditional territories in our content
- We will **not assume** that silence equals permission for cultural documentation

## Accountability & Transparency

### If We Make Mistakes

We are not indigenous, and we may make errors despite our best intentions:

1. **We will listen** if someone tells us our behavior is inappropriate
2. **We will apologize** sincerely and without defensiveness
3. **We will correct** the behavior immediately
4. **We will document** the correction for future reference

### Public Communication

All protocol requests, responses, and sponsorships will be documented on our website for transparency. We believe in **clear expectations** rather than assumptions.

## Bottom Line

Circuit Taiwan is **our expedition**. We will:

- ✅ Respect traditional territories we cross
- ✅ Follow protocols **if provided**
- ✅ Acknowledge indigenous presence and history
- ✅ Offer sponsorship opportunities to CIP and tribes
- ✅ Maintain full copyright and editorial control
- ✅ Release accessibility data as public good

We are **not**:

- ❌ A cultural documentation project
- ❌ An indigenous-led initiative
- ❌ A revenue-sharing partnership
- ❌ A collaborative storytelling platform

We are an **adaptive mobility expedition** that happens to cross indigenous territories. We seek respect, guidance, and potential sponsorship—but we retain full control of our narrative and content.

Circuit Taiwan has a 64-year-old explorer, a support driver, and an **Artificial Intelligence**.

We aren't just using AI to write emails. We are deploying it as an **Official Co-Pilot**—a silent, tireless crew member that processes real-time telemetry, translates across three languages in milliseconds, calculates wheelchair battery drain against topographical gradients, and helps us navigate the complex cultural protocols of Taiwan’s indigenous territories.

This is not a theoretical use case. This is AI running in the wild, on a moving wheelchair, on a tropical highway, with no room for hallucinations.

## The Job Description of Our AI Co-Pilot

If our AI Co-Pilot were a human, here is what their daily shift would look like:

### 1. Real-Time Safety & Telemetry Analyst
While the expedition leader focuses on the road, the AI processes continuous data streams from the AS3935 lightning sensors, smartwatch vitals, and weather APIs. It doesn't sleep. It calculates the probability of a tropical downpour intersecting with our exact GPS coordinates and triggers alerts *before* the first drop falls.

### 2. Trilingual Communications Officer
We engage with local government, indigenous tribal councils, and international media. Our AI Co-Pilot instantly translates complex logistical and cultural nuances between **English, Traditional Chinese, and French**, ensuring nothing is lost in translation and every protocol is respected.

### 3. Logistics & Energy Strategist
A powered wheelchair on a 1,200 km coastal route is an energy management exercise. The AI analyzes elevation maps, wind resistance, and solar charging forecasts to tell us exactly where we need to stop, how long we need to charge, and which route minimizes battery degradation.

### 4. Digital Archivist & Storyteller
Every evening, the AI helps structure the day's telemetry, photos, and notes into coherent, trilingual journal entries. It turns raw data into a compelling narrative for sponsors, media, and the global accessibility community.

## The Architecture: A Modular AI Stack

No single AI company does everything perfectly. That is why we are not looking for a single, exclusive "AI Sponsor." We are building a **modular, best-of-breed AI stack** optimized for extreme edge conditions, and we are seeking **category-specific partners** to power each layer.

### 🟢 Tier 1: Official AI Hardware Partner (Edge Computing)
*Target: NVIDIA, MediaTek, Advantech*
When cellular networks fail on the remote stretches of the Su-Hua Highway, the cloud disappears. We need ruggedized edge-computing hardware (like Jetson or Genio devices) to run local safety models, process sensor fusion, and keep the expedition alive offline. 
**Your Role:** Provide the silicon that keeps our AI thinking when the grid goes down.

### 🔵 Tier 2: Official AI Software Partner (LLM & Reasoning)
*Target: Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind*
For trilingual content generation, complex logistical reasoning, and cultural protocol translation, we need the world's most capable Large Language Models. We need an API that doesn't hallucinate when translating indigenous protocols or calculating energy drain.
**Your Role:** Provide the "brain" and API credits that power our high-level reasoning and communication.

### 🟣 Tier 3: Official Cloud Infrastructure Partner (Data & Telemetry)
*Target: Microsoft Azure, AWS*
A 35-day expedition generates a massive dataset of telemetry, weather, and accessibility metrics. We need a robust, scalable cloud environment to ingest, store, and process this data, syncing seamlessly with our edge hardware when connectivity returns.
**Your Role:** Provide the cloud architecture and credits to host the expedition's digital nervous system.

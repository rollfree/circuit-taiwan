Circuit Taiwan is not just an expedition. It is a data-collection initiative designed to leave a permanent, publicly accessible record of Taiwan's coastal accessibility.

Every meter of our route will be instrumented. Every gradient, surface type, charging location, and rest area will be logged. At the end of the journey, this dataset will be released under a **Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY 4.0)** license, free for anyone to use, study, or build upon.

<!--more-->

## Why Open-Source?

Accessibility is not a commercial product. It is infrastructure. 

When a wheelchair user plans a route, they need accurate, real-world data: slope angles, pavement quality, curb heights, charging station compatibility, and emergency shelter locations. Currently, this information is fragmented, anecdotal, or completely absent for Taiwan's eastern coastline.

By releasing our telemetry openly, we eliminate duplication of effort. Researchers, app developers, tourism boards, and adaptive athletes can use this data immediately, without waiting for proprietary studies or commercial mapping projects.

## What We Are Collecting

Our AI Co-Pilot and sensor array will log the following metrics continuously:

| Data Category | Metrics Collected |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Terrain** | GPS coordinates, elevation changes, slope gradients, surface material |
| **Infrastructure** | Rest stop locations, public bathroom accessibility, charging station types, shelter coverage |
| **Environmental** | Real-time temperature, humidity, UV index, wind speed, precipitation |
| **Operational** | Battery consumption rates, motor load, solar charging efficiency, connectivity gaps |

All data will be timestamped, geotagged, and cleaned for public release.

## How the Data Will Be Structured

The dataset will be published in two formats:
1. **Raw Telemetry Logs** – CSV/JSON files for developers and researchers
2. **Interactive Map Overlay** – Visual accessibility ratings for travelers and planners

We will host the dataset on our repository and mirror it to open-data platforms (e.g., Kaggle, GitHub, Taiwan Open Data Portal).

## Who Benefits

- **Adaptive Travelers**: Accurate, wheelchair-specific route planning
- **Tourism Authorities**: Infrastructure gap identification and improvement planning
- **Researchers**: Real-world mobility, energy consumption, and climate impact studies
- **Developers**: Training data for navigation apps, accessibility scoring algorithms, and AI models
- **Local Communities**: Visibility for accessible services and indigenous tourism routes

## The Sponsor Role

Sponsors who support this data initiative receive:
- **Early access** to cleaned datasets before public release
- **Co-branding** on all open-data publications and map overlays
- **Direct technical access** to raw telemetry for internal research or product development
- **CSR positioning** as a pioneer in open-access infrastructure and inclusive mobility

The estimated equivalent value of this dataset (NT$600,000) reflects the cost of independent accessibility audits, sensor deployment, data cleaning, and long-term hosting. Sponsors are not purchasing exclusive rights; they are funding the creation of a public resource that will outlive the expedition.

## Long-Term Vision

This dataset is version one. We expect it to be:
- Updated annually by future expeditions
- Integrated into national tourism and accessibility planning
- Used as training data for next-generation mobility AI
- Expanded to other regions using our open methodology

Knowledge compounds. By making this data open, we ensure that every wheelchair user, researcher, and planner who follows our route starts with a complete map, not a blank page.

# Engineering the Edge of Adaptive Mobility
### A Documented Field Research Expedition - English · 繁體中文 · Français

**First documented adaptive electric wheelchair circumnavigation of Taiwan**

Twenty-five years ago, this route was driven by car. It will now be completed from a wheelchair - with safety engineering, medical telemetry, cultural documentation, and open-source data that did not exist then. The journey is personal. The outputs are for everyone.

---

## 🔬 Core Research Dimensions

| Dimension | Primary Deliverable | Sponsor/Institution Value |
|---|---|---|
| 🩺 **Medical Telemetry** | Continuous thermoregulation, fatigue & cardiovascular dataset for a wheelchair user in sustained subtropical conditions. | Hospital research partners, adaptive sports medicine, wearable tech validation. |
| ♿ **Accessibility Mapping** | First wheelchair-level audit of Taiwan coastal highways: surface quality, shelter, charging, and gradient data. | Open-source Creative Commons database. Tourism boards, adaptive travel platforms, urban planning. |
| 🛡️ **Safety Engineering** | Field validation of an integrated lightning & weather early-warning system for vulnerable road users. | IoT/Electronics sponsors, vehicle safety R&D, open-source hardware community. |
| 🏺 **Indigenous Documentation** | Trilingual cultural archive produced with community consent and editorial authority. | French cultural institutions, anthropological research, international media. |

---

## 📍 The Route

- **Start & Finish:** Tamsui 淡水 (clockwise circuit)
- **Distance:** Approximately 1,200 km
- **Duration:** 35 riding days + static filming and cultural days
- **Period:** September - November (year TBD)
- **Critical Section:** Su-Hua Highway 蘇花改 (VERY HIGH RISK)

The Green Ghost of the Expedition

Balancing zero-emission ideals with real-world infrastructure constraints.
May 28, 2026 · 2 min | Translations:

Every expedition begins with a vision. Ours includes zero emissions, recyclable materials, and silent mobility. We call this vision “the green ghost.” It guides every decision, but like a ghost, it is not yet fully material in the landscape we are about to cross.

The Ideal vs. The Infrastructure#

The dream is straightforward: one electric assistance vehicle, powered entirely by renewable energy, leaving no carbon footprint behind. In theory, this aligns perfectly with our research ethos. In practice, Taiwan’s east and south coasts present a different reality.

ChallengeImpact on Expedition Planning
Sparse fast-charging networkThe Su-Hua Highway and Taitung coastal route lack reliable DC fast chargers
Long distances between services40-90 km stretches with no public charging infrastructure
Weather dependencySubtropical heat and typhoon seasons accelerate battery drain
Safety buffer requirementsEmergency reserves reduce usable range by 20-30 percent

The Pragmatic Compromise#

We are not abandoning sustainability. We are adapting it to reality. Our current plan uses a two-vehicle strategy:

  • West & North Coast: Fully electric van. Dense charging infrastructure, flat terrain, and urban services make this segment ideal for zero-emission travel.
  • East & South Coast: Hybrid or efficient gas vehicle. Range anxiety, elevation changes, and sparse charging stations make pure electric routing unsafe for a 35-day continuous expedition.

This is not a failure of planning. It is honest field research. Documenting where green infrastructure succeeds and where it falls short generates valuable data for adaptive mobility, tourism planning, and EV manufacturers.

Why Transparency Matters#

Investors and sponsors do not fund illusions. They fund realistic projects with measurable outcomes. By openly addressing the infrastructure gap, we:

  1. Build trust through honest reporting
  2. Create research value by mapping real-world EV readiness
  3. Offer sponsors a platform to support infrastructure development, not just vehicle placement
  4. Maintain credibility with indigenous communities and local partners who value practical solutions over performative sustainability

Progress Over Perfection#

The green ghost remains our compass. Every decision is filtered through the question: “How do we move closer to zero impact?” If that means using a hybrid vehicle on one segment to ensure safety and data continuity, we will do it. If that means partnering with charging networks to highlight infrastructure gaps, we will do it.

Sustainability is a journey, not a switch. And this expedition will document both the milestones and the missing pieces.

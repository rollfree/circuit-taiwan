The Connectivity Challenge: Proving Taiwan's Network Prowess

There is a persistent myth in the global travel and tech world that Taiwan’s East Coast and the Su-Hua Highway are “remote dead zones” with unreliable connectivity.

Circuit Taiwan is going to prove them wrong.

This year, we will lead the first-ever wheelchair circumnavigation of Taiwan. For 35 days, we will broadcast real-time telemetry, trilingual (EN/ZH/FR) logistics, and safety data directly from the road. We aren’t just traveling; we are putting Taiwan’s mobile infrastructure to the ultimate test.

We are currently seeking an Official Connectivity Partner, and we are challenging Chunghwa Telecom, Taiwan Mobile, and FarEasTone to show us—and the world—what your network can really do.

The Myth vs. The Reality#

Travel blogs often warn foreigners to download offline maps before heading to Hualien or Taitung. But the reality on the ground is vastly different.

With the completion of the Su-Hua Highway Improvement Project (蘇花改) and massive investments in 4G/5G infrastructure, Taiwan’s telecom networks are among the most robust in the world. The tunnels are lit, the coastal roads are covered, and the mountains are connected.

We want to turn this reality into a global showcase.

The Challenge: Show Us Your Prowess#

We are looking for a telecom partner confident enough in their infrastructure to let us put it on display. We will equip our support van and wheelchair with your 5G/4G mobile hotspots and unlimited data SIMs. We will push your network to its limits across 1,200 km of coastal roads, mountain tunnels, and indigenous territories.

What We Will Test & Broadcast#

  • Tunnel & Mountain Coverage: Real-time signal strength and latency logging through the Su-Hua tunnels and the Central Mountain Range.
  • High-Mobility Handover: How seamlessly your network handles 4G/5G transitions at 60 km/h on winding coastal roads.
  • Extreme Weather Resilience: Network stability during tropical downpours and high-humidity conditions.
  • Safety in Traditional Lands: Reliable connectivity when navigating the remote traditional territories of the Amis, Truku, and Paiwan nations.

This isn’t a request for charity; it’s an invitation to a highly visible, real-world marketing campaign.

Why Connectivity is a Safety Imperative#

Reliable connectivity isn’t just about uploading photos; it’s a critical safety requirement. When we are navigating remote coastal highways, a dropped connection during a sudden typhoon or earthquake isn’t an inconvenience—it’s a risk.

We need a network we can trust. We want a partner who can guarantee that when the grid is pushed to the limit, their signal holds strong.

The Bottom Line#

Everyone expects the East Coast to be a dead zone. We expect your network to prove them wrong.

If your 4G/5G infrastructure is as robust as your marketing claims, let us put it to the test. Help us prove that Taiwan’s digital infrastructure is just as advanced as its physical roads.
